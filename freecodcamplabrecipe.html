Francesinha – Porto Style

Francesinha is one of Portugal’s most iconic and indulgent dishes, originating from the city of Porto. Rich, hearty, and full of bold flavors, it is a true comfort food classic.

At its core, a Francesinha is a layered sandwich made with toasted bread, filled with steak, fresh sausage, linguiça, ham, and mortadella. The sandwich is then completely covered with melted cheese and topped with a fried egg.

What truly defines the dish is its signature sauce — a hot, flavorful blend made with beer, tomato, Port wine, whisky, spices, and chili. The sauce is poured generously over the sandwich, soaking into the layers and creating its unmistakable taste.

It is traditionally served with French fries on the side, often used to dip into the rich sauce.

Hearty, slightly spicy, and deeply satisfying, the Francesinha is more than just a sandwich — it’s a full meal experience and a symbol of Porto’s culinary identity.

Ingredients

Sauce (Serves 3 people)

  • 1 small onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Olive oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt
  • Piri-piri or chili pepper (to taste)
  • 1 beef stock cube (Knorr)
  • 1 beer (33 cl)
  • 5 tablespoons tomato pulp
  • 3 tablespoons Port wine
  • 2 tablespoons whisky
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon white wine
  • 1 coffee spoon paprika powder
  • Ground cumin (about 1/4 coffee spoon)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (dissolved in a little water)

Ingredients for Each Francesinha

  • 2 slices sandwich bread
  • 1 steak
  • Salt and pepper (Gourmarket blend)
  • 1 fresh sausage
  • 1 fresh linguiça (or packaged)
  • 6 slices of cheese
  • 1 slice ham
  • 2 slices mortadella
  • 1 egg
  • Mustard (optional, to spread on steak)

Instructions

Sauce

  1. Sauté the chopped onion and garlic in olive oil.
  2. Once golden, add the beer followed by all remaining ingredients except the cornstarch.
  3. Let it simmer for about 15 minutes.
  4. Dissolve the cornstarch in a small amount of water.
  5. Gradually add it to the sauce until the desired consistency is reached.
  6. Remove from heat, discard the bay leaf, and blend everything using a hand blender until smooth.
  7. Return to heat and adjust seasoning if necessary.

Preparing the Meats

  1. Season the steaks with salt and pepper and grill them.
  2. Grill the fresh sausage and linguiça as well, preferably sliced open lengthwise.

Assembling the Francesinha

  1. Place one slice of sandwich bread on a plate.
  2. Add one slice of ham.
  3. Add two slices of mortadella.
  4. Place the steak on top (spread lightly with mustard if desired).
  5. Add the sausage and linguiça (sliced open).
  6. Add one slice of cheese.
  7. Cover with another slice of bread.
  8. Toast lightly in a toaster.
  9. Cover with 5 slices of cheese.
  10. Place the fried egg on top.
  11. Return to the toaster or oven until the cheese is melted.

Final Step: Pour the hot sauce generously over the francesinha and serve with French fries on the side.
